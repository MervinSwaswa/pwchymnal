A Word from Our Administrator

March 2026

Dear Friends, Partners, and Community Members,

It is with a heart full of gratitude that I welcome you to the March 2026 edition of The Healing Hands. As I reflect on the past quarter, I am reminded of the incredible journey we have walked together—a journey marked by resilience, compassion, and unwavering dedication to the health and well-being of our community.

This year has already brought remarkable milestones. Our mobile clinic initiative reached over 1,200 patients in remote villages, bringing essential healthcare to those who need it most. The inauguration of our expanded Pediatric Wing represents a dream realized—a sanctuary where children can heal with dignity and hope. And behind every statistic, there is a story: a mother who safely delivered twins, a father who walks again after years, a child whose laughter now fills a home once shadowed by illness.

None of this would be possible without the dedication of our extraordinary staff, the generosity of our partners, and the trust of the families we serve. You are the hands and feet of this mission, and I am deeply honored to stand alongside you.

As we look ahead, we remain committed to expanding access, improving quality, and deepening our roots in this community we call home. Together, we are not just treating diseases—we are restoring hope, building futures, and bearing witness to the transformative power of compassionate care.

With profound gratitude and prayers,

Sr. Thandiwe Mwape

Hospital Administrator

COVER STORY

Healing Beyond Walls: Mobile Clinic Reaches 12 Villages

In our largest coordinated outreach to date, Zimba Mission Hospital deployed three fully-equipped mobile medical units across rural Southern Province, serving over 1,200 patients with comprehensive care — from maternal health screenings to malaria prevention and chronic disease management. The initiative represents a significant milestone in our commitment to bringing quality healthcare to underserved communities.

The mobile teams included doctors, nurses, and community health workers who provided consultations, vaccinations, and health education to families who would otherwise travel days to reach a clinic. "This is healthcare at its most authentic — meeting people where they are," said Dr. Joseph Mwila, Medical Director. "We saw mothers receiving prenatal care for the first time, children getting immunizations, and elders managing hypertension that had gone untreated for years."

MATERNAL HEALTH

Safe Births Initiative: 34% Reduction in Maternal Mortality

Following comprehensive training for midwives and new equipment procurement, maternal mortality in our catchment area dropped by 34%. Meet Grace, a mother of twins who delivered safely with skilled birth attendants — a testament to quality care and community trust.

NUTRITION EXCELLENCE

Community Kitchen Program Restores Health to 230 Children

Therapeutic feeding combined with local kitchen gardens has restored health to 230 malnourished children. The program, run by local volunteers, now serves as a scalable model for combating malnutrition across rural Zambia.

SURGICAL MISSION

48 Lives Transformed: International Surgical Partnership

Visiting surgeons from the UK partnered with local staff to perform 48 life-changing orthopedic and general surgeries. John, a father of four, walks again after five years of immobility thanks to a successful knee replacement.

PUBLIC HEALTH

Clean Water Initiative: 3,000 Residents Reached

Our hygiene promotion team educated 3,000 residents on cholera prevention and installed 12 hand-washing stations at local schools. A sustainable partnership for community health and disease prevention.

HOSPITAL DEVELOPMENT

New Pediatric Wing: A Sanctuary for Children's Health

This quarter marks the inauguration of our expanded Pediatric Wing, dedicated to providing specialized care for children in Southern Province. The new facility includes a dedicated neonatal unit, pediatric ICU, and child-friendly recovery spaces designed to reduce trauma and promote healing.

"Every child deserves a safe, welcoming environment to heal," said Nurse Margaret Banda, Head of Pediatrics. "This new wing represents our commitment to the youngest members of our community and the future of healthcare in Zambia."
